Our Rich Heritage

The Abacus Legacy

Dating back to ancient civilizations, the abacus represents humanity's first mechanical calculator. Originating around 2400 BC in Babylon, it spread across cultures, evolving into different forms like the Chinese suanpan and Japanese soroban. This ancient tool continues to be relevant today, developing mental calculation abilities and spatial reasoning in young learners.

Vedic Mathematics Heritage

Vedic Mathematics, reconstructed from ancient Indian texts by Sri Bharati Krishna Tirthaji in the early 20th century, represents a systematic approach to calculations. Based on 16 sutras and 13 sub-sutras, it offers powerful mental calculation techniques that align with modern mathematical understanding.
