What is Vedic Mathematics?

Vedic Mathematics is an ancient Indian system of mathematics that was rediscovered from the Vedas between 1911 and 1918 by Sri Bharati Krishna Tirthaji. The system consists of 16 Sutras (formulas) and 13 sub-sutras (sub-formulas) which can be used for problems involving arithmetic, algebra, geometry, calculus, and conics.

Benefits of Learning Vedic Mathematics:

  • • Faster calculations and mental math abilities
  • • Enhanced problem-solving skills
  • • Improved concentration and memory
  • • Reduced dependency on calculators
  • • Increased confidence in mathematics

Quick Vedic Math Tricks

Multiply by 11

To multiply a two-digit number by 11:

  1. 1. Add the two digits
  2. 2. Place the sum between the digits
  3. Example: 43 × 11 = 473
  4. (4 + 3 = 7, place between 4 and 3)

Square Numbers Ending in 5

To square a number ending in 5:

  1. 1. Multiply the first digit by its next number
  2. 2. Add 25 to the end
  3. Example: 85² = (8 × 9 = 72)25 = 7225
